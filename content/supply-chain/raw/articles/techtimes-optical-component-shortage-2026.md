# AI Data Center Optical Component Shortage: Nvidia's $4B Laser Lockup Pushes Rivals Past 2027
*Source: techtimes.com | Author: Kyle Belmonte | Date: 2026-05-27*

The AI infrastructure boom has created its next supply chain crisis. Having already driven shortages in GPUs, HBM, and advanced packaging substrates, the buildout of AI data centers has now produced a severe crunch in optical communications components — the high-speed lasers, fiber optic cables, and transceivers that form the nervous system connecting racks inside AI training clusters.

According to McKinsey analysis, production of 800G transceivers will fall 40-60% short of demand through 2027, with shortfalls of 30-40% likely for 1.6T devices through 2029.

## 57% Market Surge

TrendForce projects the global market for AI-focused optical transceivers will expand from $16.5 billion in 2025 to $26 billion in 2026 — growth of more than 57% year-over-year. Shipments of 800G and higher transceivers are forecast to surge from 24 million units in 2025 to nearly 63 million in 2026 — a 2.6-fold jump in a single year.

## Nvidia Locks Up EML Laser Supply

At the heart of the crisis is the electro-absorption modulated laser (EML). EMLs combine a continuous light source and a high-speed modulator on a single chip, encoding data onto light signals at speeds up to 200 gigabits per lane. Fewer than five companies worldwide manufacture them at commercial scale: Lumentum, Coherent, Mitsubishi, Sumitomo, and Broadcom.

On March 2, 2026, Nvidia committed $2 billion each to Lumentum and Coherent — a $4 billion lockup of EML laser supply — along with additional investments in Scintil Photonics and Ayar Labs. Lead times at key EML suppliers have stretched beyond 2027 for any buyer who is not Nvidia.

The alternative path — continuous-wave lasers paired with silicon photonics chips — is itself under pressure. CW laser production faces long equipment delivery cycles and strict reliability standards.

## Fiber Shortage

AI-focused data centers need approximately 36 times more fiber than traditional CPU server racks. Data center fiber demand grew roughly 76% year-on-year in 2025. North American demand growth is projected at 22-25% in 2026, while supply expansion trails at 12-19%. Lead times have stretched to 20 weeks for large-volume buyers and up to a year for smaller purchasers. Manufacturing optical fiber preforms takes 18-24 months to expand.

## Hyperscaler Supply Agreements

Meta signed a $6 billion supply agreement with Corning in January 2026. Nvidia separately invested $300 million in Corning to fund three new fiber manufacturing plants. Fujikura announced a 300 billion yen ($1.88 billion) investment to triple production capacity across Japan and the US.

## Chinese Suppliers

Innolight and Eoptolink (both China-based) supply approximately 60% of Nvidia's 800G module demand. Seven of the top ten global optical module suppliers in 2024 were Chinese companies. However, export controls restrict access to certain epitaxial growth equipment needed to scale production of EMLs and DSP chips.

## Capacity Timeline

Lumentum is the only supplier shipping 200G-per-lane EMLs at volume. The specialized indium phosphide fabrication required for EML lasers involves yields ranging from 15% to 50%. LightCounting projects InP laser shortages would begin to ease by mid-2026, but does not forecast a full cycle downturn.
