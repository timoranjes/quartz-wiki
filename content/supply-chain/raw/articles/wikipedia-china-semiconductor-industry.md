# Semiconductor Industry in China
*Source: Wikipedia (en.wikipedia.org) | Accessed: 2026-05-29*

## Overview

China is the world's largest semiconductor market in terms of consumption. In 2020, China represented 53.7% of worldwide chip sales ($239.45 billion out of $446.1 billion), but over 83% ($199.7 billion) was imported from multinational suppliers.

## Domestic Production Initiatives

- In 2022, China announced a Made in China 2025 goal of 70% domestic production.
- China leads the world in number of new fabs under construction: 8 out of 19 worldwide in 2021.
- Total installed capacity of Chinese-owned chipmakers increased from 2.96 million wpm (2020) to 3.57 million wpm (2021).
- In 2025, Morgan Stanley estimated China had 41% self-sufficiency in AI chips, up from 10% in 2020, expected to reach 86% by 2030.

## Key Players

**IDMs:** YMTC (NAND flash), CXMT (DRAM), Hangzhou Silan Microelectronics
**Pure-play foundries:** SMIC (largest in mainland China, 5th globally at 5.3% share), Hua Hong Semiconductor, Wingtech
**Fabless:** HiSilicon (Huawei), Loongson, UNISOC, Hygon Information Technology
**OSAT:** JCET, Huatian Technology, Tongfu Microelectronics

## Export Controls and Response

- October 2022: US announced major export restrictions on AI and semiconductor technologies to China.
- January 2023: Controls made multilateral with US, Japan, Netherlands agreement.
- December 2023: China prohibited use of Intel/AMD CPUs for government PCs and servers; approved 18 domestic processors (Loongson, Phytium).
- September 2024: China announced two new DUV lithography machines (193nm at <65nm resolution; 248nm at 110nm resolution).
- December 2025: China reportedly completed EUV lithography prototype in Shenzhen, expected to produce working chips 2028-2030.
- May 2026: US approved 10 Chinese companies (Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, JD.com) for purchasing NVIDIA H200 chips.
