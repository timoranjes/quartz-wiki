# TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)

TSMC is the world's largest contract chipmaker and the dominant player in advanced semiconductor manufacturing.

## Market Position

- Controls ~70% of global advanced chip manufacturing
- Manufactures ~85% of advanced AI processors
- Q1 2026: 58% profit jump to NT$572.48 billion ($17.8 billion)
- High-performance computing accounts for 61% of sales

## Key Technologies

- **Advanced nodes:** 3nm and 2nm production, with demand ~3x available capacity
- **CoWoS packaging:** The industry's primary advanced packaging solution for AI accelerators, sold out through 2025 and into 2026
- **HBM demand:** TSMC confirms HBM bit demand growing exponentially, tight supply-demand balance through 2026

## Capacity Expansion

- Investing $165 billion in US expansion: 5 new fabs and 2 packaging facilities in Arizona
- Additional facilities in Germany and Japan
- Combined with significant capex expansion for CoWoS and advanced nodes
- However, even with massive investment, wafer output is still not enough to support AI demand

## Geopolitical Context

- Taiwan concentration creates significant geopolitical risk
- Diversification efforts face lengthy qualification cycles for new facilities
- Industry bifurcating: US/Taiwan/Japan alliance controlling advanced nodes, China building domestic mature node capacity

## Relationships

- Primary manufacturer for [[nvidia]] GPUs and most AI accelerators
- Key CoWoS packaging provider for the AI industry
- Dependent on [[asml]] EUV lithography equipment for advanced nodes
- Supply chain constraints across [[cowos-packaging]] and [[hbm-memory]]
