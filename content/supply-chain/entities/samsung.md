# Samsung Electronics

Samsung is the world's largest NAND flash memory producer and the #2 HBM supplier, with significant foundry operations.

## HBM Position

- **HBM market share:** 22% (Q3 2025, per Astute Group)
- Targeting 30%+ share in 2026, aggressively closing the gap with SK Hynix
- Began mass production of HBM4 in February 2026 simultaneously with SK Hynix
- Raising HBM prices by high-teens to low-twenties percent in 2026 contracts

## HBF Strategy

- Chose solo development path for HBF (vs. SK Hynix's co-development with SanDisk)
- Going forward, companies with HBM stacking expertise will have structural advantage in HBF

## Other Positions

- World's largest NAND flash memory producer (two plants in Xi'an account for 42.5% of total production capacity)
- Memory division: "Customers' demand for next year will exceed our supply" — Samsung Memory

## Relationships

- Competing with [[sk-hynix]] and [[micron]] for HBM market share
- Foundry operations compete with [[tsmc]] for advanced node manufacturing
- Memory products supply [[nvidia]] and other AI GPU makers
